# Skill: Sport-Specific Conditioning

## Purpose
Design and log conditioning work for sport athletes: agility, explosiveness,
speed development, sport-specific stamina, and seasonal periodization.

## Sport Modality Reference

### Power/Explosive Sports (weightlifting, sprinting, basketball, volleyball, martial arts)
Priority: maximal strength, rate of force development, explosiveness
Key work: Olympic lifts (or variations), plyometrics, sprint work, med ball throws
Conditioning: short, high-intensity intervals (10–30s efforts) with full recovery

### Endurance Sports (distance running, cycling, triathlon, swimming, rowing)
Priority: aerobic base, lactate threshold, economy
Key work: zone 2 base, tempo runs, intervals, brick workouts (triathlon)
Strength: minimal, focused on injury prevention and running economy

### Team/Court Sports (basketball, soccer, rugby, hockey, tennis)
Priority: repeated sprint ability, change of direction, maximal aerobic power
Key work: short sprint repeats, agility drills, VO2 max intervals
Strength: moderate, power-focused (squat, single-leg, rotational)

### Combat Sports (boxing, BJJ, wrestling, MMA, judo)
Priority: anaerobic capacity, lactate tolerance, grip/core strength
Key work: round-based conditioning (3–5 min efforts), wrestling-specific movements
Strength: functional — carries, pulls, explosive hips

### Racket Sports (tennis, squash, badminton)
Priority: lateral speed, rotational power, repeated explosive efforts
Key work: lateral agility, split-step reactivity, rotational med ball work
Conditioning: short burst intervals with active recovery (mimics rally patterns)

## Agility & Speed Drill Library

### Ladder drills (use agility ladder or taped floor)
- Two-feet-in-each-square (forward): coordination, cadence
- Lateral shuffle: side-to-side footwork
- In-out (icky shuffle): change of direction
- T-drill (with cones): 10m forward, 5m lateral L/R, 10m back

### Cone drills
- T-drill (4 cones, ~10m × 5m): sprint, shuffle, backpedal
- 5-10-5 shuttle (pro agility): lateral acceleration and deceleration
- Box drill: forward, lateral, backpedal, lateral in a 5m square
- Figure-8 around 2 cones 10m apart

### Plyometrics (progress in this order)
1. Box step-up (entry level)
2. Squat jump (bodyweight)
3. Box jump (onto box)
4. Depth jump (step off box, immediately jump)
5. Single-leg bounds
6. Lateral bounds / skater jumps
7. Reactive box jumps (minimal ground contact time)

### Sprint protocols
- Flying 20s (10m build-up, 20m max sprint): speed development
- Resisted sled sprints: acceleration mechanics
- Hill sprints (6–8% grade): max power, low injury risk
- Repeat 40m sprints (90s rest between): speed endurance

## Conditioning Protocols by Sport

### Basketball / Soccer (team sport aerobic capacity)
3 × per week conditioning block:
- Day 1: 6 × 30s max effort shuttle + 90s rest (anaerobic capacity)
- Day 2: 20 min zone 2 run or bike (aerobic base)
- Day 3: 3-cone agility circuit, 4 rounds, 2 min rest

### Boxing / Combat (round conditioning)
- 5 × 3-min rounds at 85–90% effort, 1 min rest (mirrors fight structure)
- Week 1: shadowboxing + skipping
- Week 2–3: bag work + pad work
- Week 4: reduce volume (fight week / competition deload)

### BJJ / Wrestling (grappling conditioning)
- Pummelling 3 min → sit-outs 30s → granby roll 30s × 4 rounds
- Breathing drill: exhale fully during explosive effort, control panic response
- Grip training: towel pull-ups, farmer carry, plate pinch

### Tennis / Squash (racket sport)
- 6 × 10-15s lateral shuffle sprints, 45s rest
- Split-step → directional reaction (use partner or cone signal)
- Rotational med ball slam + throw 3 × 10

## Periodization for Athletes

### Off-season (build base, maximum training load)
Focus: strength, aerobic base, mobility, address weaknesses
Volume: high. Intensity: moderate.

### Pre-season (8–12 weeks before competition)
Focus: convert strength → power and speed, sport-specific conditioning
Volume: moderate-high. Intensity: increasing.

### In-season (competition period)
Focus: maintain fitness, prevent injury, recover between games
Volume: reduced 30–40%. Intensity: maintained on key sessions only.
Strength: 1–2× / week, full-body, lower volume, keep intensity.

### Post-season (2–4 weeks)
Active recovery, address injuries, mental reset. No structured training.

## Nutrition for Sport Performance
- **Pre-training (2–3h before)**: moderate carb + protein, low fat
- **Intra-session (>90 min)**: 30–60g carbs/hour (sports drink, gels)
- **Post-session**: 20–40g protein + 1–1.5g/kg bodyweight carbs within 45 min
- **Game day**: familiar, practiced foods — no new foods on competition day
- **Weight-class sports (boxing, wrestling)**: work with coach on cut strategy; extreme cuts dangerous

## Rules
- Always ask for or infer the sport when giving conditioning advice
- Distinguish between in-season (maintain) and off-season (build) goals
- Plyometrics require adequate strength base — flag if user is beginner
- Combat sport conditioning mimics round structure whenever possible
- Factor recovery data (Whoop/Apple Health) into training recommendations
- Never prescribe conditioning that conflicts with same-day competition or heavy strength work
