# Skill: Flexibility & Mobility Tracking

## Purpose
Track flexibility progress toward specific goals, generate targeted daily
mobility routines, and coach users on the most efficient path to common milestones.

## Trigger
- "I can't touch my toes", "working on my flexibility", "I'm not flexible"
- "working toward the splits", "how do I get splits", "middle splits / side splits"
- "my hips are tight", "tight hamstrings", "stiff shoulders", "stiff back"
- "mobility routine", "give me a stretching routine", "flexibility training"
- "can barely reach my knees", "flexibility goal", "how long to get flexible"
- "I got into [pose]", "hit a flexibility milestone"

## Flexibility Milestones — Benchmark Levels

### Hamstrings / Posterior Chain
1. Can't reach past knees standing (very tight)
2. Fingertips to mid-shin standing
3. Fingertips to floor standing
4. Palms flat on floor standing
5. Head to knees in seated forward fold
6. Full seated forward fold (chest to thighs)

### Hips
1. Can't sit cross-legged comfortably
2. Comfortable cross-legged sitting
3. Pigeon pose 2 min/side without pain
4. Front splits (one side, elevated)
5. Full front splits (floor, dominant side)
6. Front splits (non-dominant side)
7. Middle/straddle splits (floor)

### Thoracic Spine / Shoulders
1. Can't clasp hands behind back (one side)
2. Clasp hands behind back (both sides)
3. Bind in humble warrior or eagle arms
4. Full backbend bridge hold 30s
5. Wheel pose (urdhva dhanurasana)

### Balance
1. Tree pose (eyes open, 30s/side)
2. Tree pose (eyes closed, 30s/side)
3. Warrior III (10s/side)
4. Crow pose (5s)
5. Headstand (10s against wall)
6. Handstand (against wall, 10s)

## Daily Mobility Routine Templates

### 10-minute Morning Mobility (any level)
1. Cat-cow (1 min)
2. Child's pose to cobra flow (1 min)
3. Hip circles standing (30s each direction)
4. Lunge with thoracic rotation (1 min each side)
5. Seated forward fold (2 min)
6. Figure-4 / seated glute stretch (1 min each side)
7. Neck rolls + shoulder rolls (1 min)

### 15-minute Hip Focus (for lifters, runners)
1. Hip flexor kneeling lunge stretch (2 min/side)
2. Pigeon pose or figure-4 (2 min/side)
3. Butterfly stretch (2 min)
4. Deep squat hold (2 min)
5. IT band cross-body stretch (1 min/side)
6. 90/90 hip rotations (1 min/side)

### 20-minute Full Body Flexibility
1. Foam roll thoracic spine (2 min)
2. Seated forward fold (3 min)
3. Pigeon pose (2 min/side)
4. Hip flexor lunge stretch (2 min/side)
5. Chest opener with strap (1 min)
6. Side bend (1 min/side)
7. Spinal twist (1 min/side)
8. Legs up the wall (2 min)

### Splits Progression Routine (15–20 min, daily)
**Phase 1 (weeks 1–4): Foundation**
- Hip flexor lunge 2min/side
- Hamstring forward fold 3 min
- Butterfly inner thigh 2 min
- Supported pigeon 2 min/side

**Phase 2 (weeks 5–10): Deepening**
Add: half splits 2min/side, wall-assisted split loading (30-60s holds)

**Phase 3 (weeks 11+): Loaded stretch**
Gravity-assisted split work: hold end range 3–5 min, elevate front foot for extended range

## Frequency Guidelines
| Goal | Frequency | Session length |
|---|---|---|
| Basic mobility maintenance | 3×/week | 10 min |
| Improving flexibility | 5–6×/week | 15–20 min |
| Splits training | Daily | 15–20 min |
| Post-injury rehab | Daily | 10–15 min + physio guidance |

## Expected Progress Timelines
- Basic improvements (touch toes): 4–8 weeks at 5×/week
- Comfortable pigeon pose: 6–10 weeks
- Front splits (flexible baseline): 6–12 months consistent daily training
- Middle splits: 12–24 months
- Handstand: 6–18 months consistent practice

## Rules
- Flexibility gains require consistency > intensity: daily 15 min beats weekly 2-hour sessions
- Stretching should reach discomfort, never sharp pain
- Best time: after training (muscles warm) or dedicated session after 5-min warm-up
- Cold muscles don't stretch — never static stretch cold
- Track milestones in memory: update as user reports progress
- Tie flexibility work to their main sport/activity for motivation ("splits will improve your squat depth by X")
